Welcome to Kokshetau

Kokshetau (Kazakh: Көкшетау, romanized: Kökşetau; [kɵkʂʲetɑw] (listen), lit. 'Blueish Mountain/Smoky-Blue Mountain'; Russian: Кокшета́у, IPA: [kəkʂɛtaʊ]), formerly known as Kokchetav (Russian: Кокчета́в, [kəktɕʲetav]; the official name between 1868–1993) is a city in northern Kazakhstan and the capital of Akmola Region, which stretches along the southern shore of Lake Kopa, lying in the north of Kokshetau Hills, a northern subsystem of the Kokshetau Uplands (Saryarka) and the southern edge of the Ishim Plain. It is named after the Mount Kokshe. Earlier, it was the administrative center of Kokshetau Region, which was abolished in 1997.
The city is considered to have been founded in 1824 as a military fortress, while it was granted city status in 1895. It was the centre of Kokshetau Region, which was abolished in 1997. It is well known for its nature and tourist sites, such as Burabay and Zerendi, among others. The city's main football team is FC Okzhetpes. Kokshetau is about 185 km (115 mi) from Petropavl, 300 km (190 mi) northwest of the national capital Astana along the A1, 318 km (198 mi) from Omsk along the A13, and 384 km (239 mi) from Kostanay. The city is served by Kokshetau Airport (KOV).
